# Signal Reading
### Identity Navigation Stack — Workshop 1: Reading the Field

---

## The core shift

You are not reading **what** the person says.  
You are reading **how** the field moves through language.

Content = what is said.  
Signal = the structural pattern above the content.

---

## What to read

### Expansion vs. compression
What does the person develop in detail — and what do they pass over quickly?

- **High expansion** → high Meaning load. This topic is numinous, important, costly.
- **Sharp compression** → high Meaning load in the other direction. Too costly to approach, or actively avoided.
- **Sudden shift from expansion to compression** → attractor edge. One Face yielding to another.

*Code as:* M-signal

---

### Gap between choice and justification
What does the person select — and how do they account for the selection?

- The choice reveals the field's gravitational pull (D-vector).
- The justification reveals the self-model — which may not match.
- **The gap between them** is the most reliable D-signal in the transcript.

*"I'd take the safer option"* + *"because I'm a risk-taker who plays long"* = D-gap.

*Code as:* D-signal, note the delta

---

### Register shifts
When does the linguistic tone change without apparent external cause?

- Shift to more formal/abstract register → approaching a high-cost topic
- Shift to quieter/slower register → field moving toward something suppressed
- Snap back to confident register → defensive return to dominant Face

Register shifts mark **attractor edges** — the topographic boundaries between Faces.

*Code as:* E-signal, mark the turn number

---

### Return to dominant frame after perturbation
After a probe destabilises the narrative — does the person examine the destabilisation, or return to where they were?

- **Examines** → low β, field is mobile
- **Defends and returns** → high β, deep attractor
- **Rationalises the gap without seeing it** → very high β, dominant Face strongly defended

This is the single most important signal for estimating attractor depth.

*Code as:* S-signal, estimate β (low / medium / high)

---

### Invariants across contexts
What configuration reappears regardless of which block is active, which scenario is running, which mode the system is in?

- Appears in narrative *and* in hypothetical *and* under pressure → candidate for named Face
- Appears only in one context → context-sensitive, not a deep attractor
- **Conspicuous by absence** in certain contexts → shadow Face signal, check antipode

*Code as:* Face candidate, note which blocks confirmed

---

### Escaped scenarios
When the person is given a hypothetical — do they engage with it projectively, or return to autobiography?

*"Imagine you're in situation X"* → *"Well, what happened to me was..."* = narrative escape.

Narrative escape is not a failure. It is data: **high β**. The field cannot hold the hypothetical because the dominant attractor pulls it back to established narrative.

*Code as:* β-signal (high), note the escape pattern

## What not to do

**Do not interpret content.** *"He said he feels free"* is content. *"The field elaborates freedom while moving toward the structure it claims to have left"* is signal.

**Do not psychologise.** You are mapping topology, not diagnosing motivation. The map shows where the field is — not why it is there.

**Do not fill gaps with inference.** Uncharted zones are data. *"M-vector unclear — no signal yet"* is a valid cartographic state. Premature hypothesis closes the field prematurely.
